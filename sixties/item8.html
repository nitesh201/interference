This pamphlet attempted to unite protestors for a 1969 gathering in Chicago to protest the Vietnam War and express solidarity for the Chicago Eight, a group on trial for their part in the 1968 Democratic National Convention protests in Chicago. The large scale 1968 protests, organized by the Youth International Party (Yippies), Student for a Democratic Society (SDS) and MOBE (National Mobilization Committee To End The War In Vietnam), resulted in a police riot that raged for much of the week and dominated the climate of the convention. In the widely publicized trial that followed, the group of protest organizers who became known as the Chicago Eight (including Yippies Abbie Hoffman and Jerry Rubin, SDS Port Huron Statement author Tom Hayden, and Black Panther founder Bobby Seale) were charged with conspiracy, inciting a riot, and multiple counts of contempt of court. All charges were eventually overturned. The 1968 DNC protests could be considered the last substantial action of the SDS; it had begun to split due to infighting between factions concerned about women's rights, black self determination, and a shift from campus organizing to revolutionizing the working class.
